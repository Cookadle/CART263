## (Virtual Computer)
[https://yatsukki.github.io/cart263/Project2/draw.html]

I like how the project is illustrated as if the user was running a virtual computer. It could have been a simple app, but the VM look makes it feel more personal and interactive. It really gives that feeling of being 9 again, discovering a computer and clicking around to see what everything does. That nostalgic aspect works very well and feels polished.
The different features, like the drawing tool and music player, also reinforce the idea of a full  computer system. The drawing app gives the impression it was the most developed and polished, while the other parts feel simpler in comparison. Because of that, the experience does feels a bit lacking.
For example, the start button being clickable is a good idea, but since nothing happens when clicking it, it feels unfinished. Adding even a small menu or animation would make it more satisfying.

## (Live Tamagotchi Digi Chiika)
[https://mewmewpewpew.github.io/Project1/]

I love how the concept of Tamagotchi has come full circle from being digital toys on our backpacks to an interactive web page with its own dedicated space. As I play with the buttons, some of them become completely blank, which starts to feel confusing for both me and the “pet.” At the same time, I hear a whispering dog in the background, along with music, and ASCII art suddenly appears. Altogether, it feels a bit messy and overwhelming.
I can see how the user is meant to interact, but over time it feels more like I’m just clicking buttons and waiting for something to happen rather than actively controlling the outcome. Maybe that’s intentional,it reflects the limited control we often have with Tamagotchi toys but it still creates a sense of temporary engagement rather than sustained interaction.
I do love the design of the page. The Y2K elements and early 2000s gaming nostalgia are beautifully incorporated and also visually appealing. However, the experience feels more like layers waiting to be discovered rather than something the user is actively shaping. 

## [Beat it up] 
(https://aydanmcgrail.github.io/cart263_FinalProject/)

First, I love the game concept. It’s very original and interesting. However, I do find it a bit frustrating that the controls are explained while you are already in the game. It makes the start confusing and not very user-friendly. It took me around five tries to understand what was going on, and even then I had to check the README, which was quite minimal. I ended up spending more time figuring out the mechanics than actually playing.

I find it really interesting how the opponent takes over your controls, forcing you to constantly check whether your inputs are still yours. It feels like a metaphor for social media platforms like Meta, where you are pushed to consume content and lose some control. In that sense, the confusion and repetition might be intentional, reflecting how users gradually understand these systems over time and could justify the reasoning behind the controls being explained that way.

The JavaScript files seem well organized and show that a lot of thought went into the project especially after reading the artist statement. The use of 3D models is also impressive and adds depth to the experience. It honestly feels like something that could have existed on older Flash game websites like Miniclip which often feature games with strong concept and visuals, but slightly lacking in user experience and clarity.
