#[Interactive bike garage](https://emilebedard.github.io/cart263/Projects/project1/Interactive_Bike_Garage/parts_registry.html)
I find Emile idea so cool,I even put my partner who bike on it. First the little animation sliding in a out is a cool detail. It really makes it feel like an app. I like how intuitive it is,the user doesn’t have to figure out what is the goal of the web or how it works. I enjoy the option to input many bikes in the system,the fact that each bike as their own colour and that you can also add parts!This is perfect example of web 2.0 where the user mould its experiences based on their own needs and not the programmer shaping the user experience fully. Emile also broke down a complex system such as a bike and simplified for users. I appreciate the fact that Emile took his own advice and expertise to shared with others users.


#[Snake Game](https://yaxuanpang.github.io/cart263/topics/project1/)
I am not a fan of snake like game because of its endless without a goal. I like that this one does have an actual goal. I think the project itself look so much fun because of how it was designed and also the rules/logic of the game. The beauty of this project is collaboration .I love how art is included in the process such as the colours or background. The moving background remind me of the old DVD logo bouncing from border to border. I do find it a bit lengthy to get to 50 so I actually let my friends played it. I think a goal less high to achieve would help enjoy it. On the other hand,I don’t feel like there was much integration of Dom elements per say beyond the snake and the obstacles randomly spawning .I wish it was a bit more interactive beyond a snake game such as answering a random maths question once you reach a certain score or what not. Its a great concept but I know I could do the same concept with JS P5 and it would function as well without needing DOM elements.

#[Sandbox](https://seangregoryv8.github.io/cart263/projects/1/)
I love the idea of making a sandbox. It is quite hard  to make a classic sandbox with liquids and sand so the fact that he didn’t let that stop him and decided to use cube instead is quite original. I love the humour he integrated such as getting redirect to a YouTube video if you find the secret number and the playful message when you try to close the terminal. I love that he also implemented a controls guide that can be triggered through commands. I love the idea of using cubes but I wish more than just cube was added,maybe some falling balls or other shapes would have been fun. there's a lack of diversity when it comes to what the user can do beyond moving creating etc. Some animation would have been fun like adding bouncy balls. It a fun work but I feel like there is so much more options we can add  before I can call it a proper sandbox.
